Teaching Machine Learning Concepts

I created an introductory worksheet on Information Gain for high school students exploring classifiers in machine learning. The goal was to simplify complex concepts like feature selection, decision trees, and the importance of informative attributes, so that beginners could grasp them intuitively.

To make the content engaging, I included small exercises where students could calculate Information Gain on simple datasets and visually see how decision tree splits work. This hands-on approach helped reinforce abstract concepts and made learning more interactive.

Sharing ML knowledge with beginners has been incredibly rewarding. It not only allowed me to consolidate my own understanding of the material but also gave me insight into the challenges learners face when approaching advanced topics for the first time.

This project highlighted the importance of clear communication in STEM education. By translating technical ideas into accessible exercises and visual examples, I was able to spark curiosity and inspire students to explore machine learning further.
